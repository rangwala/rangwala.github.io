About

I am a Senior Manager of Applied Science at Amazon Web Services (AWS), where I lead science teams across AutoML, Bedrock Guardrails, Model Customization, and Graph ML. My teams build and ship foundation models and enterprise AI solutions at scale — including AutoGluon, Chronos (600M+ downloads), Amazon Bedrock Guardrails, and GraphRAG.

Previously, I was a Professor of Computer Science at George Mason University (2008–2024), where I served as Interim Chair and held the Lawrence Cranberry Faculty Fellowship. I hold a Ph.D. (2008) and M.S. (2005) from the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities and a B.E. from VJTI Mumbai (2003). My research spans machine learning, data mining, federated learning, and graph neural networks, with funding from NSF, NIH, DARPA, and Google.
