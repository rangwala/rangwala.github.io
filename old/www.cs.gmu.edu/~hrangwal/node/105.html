Readings

21 Aug 2009
Posted by rangwala

Here is a list of topics and the readings associated with it.

  1. Introductory Readings
    1. Machine learning in bioinformatics by Larrañaga et. al
    2. Top 10 Data Mining Algorithms by Wu et. al
    3. Microbiology for Computer Scientists by Hunter et. al
    4. What is a hidden markov model ? by Eddy et. al
    5. Bayesian methods in bioinformatics and computational systems biology by Wilkison et. al
  2. Kernels in Computational Biology
    1. Support Vector Machines and Kernels for Computational Biology by Ben-Hur et. al
    2. A primer on Kernel Methods by Vert et. al
    3. Support Vector Machines and Applications in Computational Biology by Noble et. al
  3. Remote Homology Detection and Fold Recognition
    1. The WWWH of remote homolog detection: The state of the art by Fariselli et. al
    2. Profile-based direct kernels for remote homology detection and fold
      recognition       by Rangwala et. al
    3. Protein homology detection by HMM–HMM comparison by Söding et. al.
    4. Hidden Markov models for detecting remote protein homologies by
      Karplus et. al
    5. Protein ranking: from local to global structure in the protein similarity network by Weston et. al
    6. Building multiclass classifiers for remote homology detection and fold recognition by Rangwala et. al
    7. Combining classifiers for improved classification of proteins from sequence or structure by Melvin et. al
  4. Chemoinformatics
    1. Prediction methods and databases within chemoinformatics: emphasis on drugs and drug candidates. by Jónsdóttir et. al.
    2. Frequent Sub-structure Based Approaches for Classifying Chemical Compounds by Deshpande et. al
    3. The pharmacophore kernel for virtual screening with support vector machines. by Mahe et. al
  5. Protein-Ligand Interaction
    1. The Druggable Genome by Hopkins et. al
    2. Exploring Biology with small molecules by Stockwell
    3. Navigating chemical space for biology and medicine by Lipinski et. al
    4. Chemogenomics: structuring the drug discovery process to gene families by Harris et. al
    5. Protein-ligand interaction prediction: an improved chemogenomics approach by Jacob et. al
    6. A unified statistical model to support local sequence order independent similarity searching for ligand-binding sites and its application to genome-based drug discovery by Xie et. al
    7. Prediction of sub-cavity binding preferences using an adaptive physicochemical structure representation by Wallach et. al
    8. Multi-Assay-based Structure-Activity-Relationship Models: Improving Structure-Activity-Relationship Models by Incorporating Activity Information from Related Targets by Ning et. al
  6. Interaction Sites
    1. Progress and challenges in predicting protein-protein interaction sites by Ezkurdia et. al
    2. ProSAT: PROtein reSidue Annotation Toolkit by Rangwala et. al
  7. Protein-Protein Interaction Networks
    1. Deciphering protein-protein interactions. Part I. Experimental techniques and databases. by Shoemaker et. al
    2. Network-based prediction of protein function by Sharan et. al
    3. An automated method for finding molecular complexes in large protein interaction networks by Bader et. al
    4. An ensemble framework for clustering protein–protein interaction networks by Asur et. al
    5. Kernel methods for predicting protein–protein interactions by Ben-Hur et. al
  8. Comparing Metagenomic Samples
  9. Protein Function Prediction
    1. Kernel-based data fusion and its application to protein function prediction in yeast by Lancreit et. al
    2. Predicting gene function in a hierarchical context with an ensemble of classifiers by Guan et. al
    3. Hierarchical multi-label prediction of gene function by Barutcuoglu et. al
