Team of Undergraduates and High School Students join the group for summer.

19 Jun 2013
Posted by rangwala

We have two undergraduates from George Mason University and three high school students join the group to work on developing computational models for metagenome analysis and motif detection. Watch this space for all the fun and cool work they do over the summer!

Liam Dannaher is a Computer Science Undergraduate at George Mason University. He is currently employed at Naval Research Lab in DC as a Flight Software Engineer. Computer science fields that he is most interested in include Computer Vision, Data Mining, and Cyber Security. He is working on motif detection algorithms and is also mentored by Prof. Jessica Lin
Anisha Kolla is a Sophomore, pursuing her B.S. in Computer Science at George Mason University. She is working this summer on developing clustering algorithms for metagenome analysis.
Hariank Muthakana is a student at Thomas Jefferson High School. He is interested in the math and the computational sciences, and plans to major in computer science. In the future he is aiming to work on research in the field or in the software industry. He is co-mentored by Prof. Daniel Barbara
Ashwin Sekar is a rising senior at Poolesville High School in the Science Math and Computer Science house. He has a strong affection for Computer Science since an early age, and hope to further his experiences in Applied Computer Science through this project.
Ashley Zhang is currently a rising sophomore at Langley High School. She is on the varsity debate team, an avid violinist, and also has participated in the science fair.
